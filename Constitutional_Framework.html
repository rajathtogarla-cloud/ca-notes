1. Supremacy of the Constitution

The Supreme Law of the Land. All authorities—Parliament, Executive (CBDT/CBIC), and Judiciary—must act within its four corners.

Verdict: If any tax law or action violates the Constitution → It is VOID.

2. Article 265

The Core Taxing Principle

"No tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law."

"No Tax"

Scope
  • Direct Taxes
  • Indirect Taxes
  • Fees mislabelled as tax

"Shall be Levied"

Imposition

"Or Collected"

Recovery

Collection refers to actual recovery.

Crucial Point:
Even if the Levy is valid, if the Collection method is illegal, it is Unconstitutional.

"Authority of Law"

The Source

3. Article 246 &
The 7th Schedule

Article 246 divides law-making power. It is the distribution mechanism. The 7th Schedule is the "Structural Backbone" containing the detailed lists.

Vital for Income Tax

Entry 82, Union List

"Taxes on income other than agricultural income"

  • ✓ Parliament alone can levy Income Tax.
  • ✗ States are constitutionally barred.
Article 248: Residuary Powers

If it's not in any list, it belongs to Parliament (e.g., Digital Economy Tax).

Why can't Parliament tax Agricultural Income?

Because Entry 82 explicitly excludes it, and Entry 46 of the State List explicitly includes it. It is a constitutional exclusion, not a policy choice.

Fundamental Limitations & Rights

14

Equality Before Law

Article 14 forbids arbitrary classification.

Impact on Tax

Tax laws cannot discriminate without "Intelligible Differentia". Arbitrary tax rates or classes can be struck down.

19

Freedom of Trade

Article 19(1)(g): Right to practice any profession.

Impact on Tax

Taxation cannot be confiscatory. Excessive tax that destroys the right to business is unconstitutional.

300A

Right to Property

No person shall be deprived of property save by authority of law.

Impact on Tax

Illegal recovery or attachment of assets without following procedure is unconstitutional.

The Guardians

Where do you go when the Executive oversteps?

141

Judicial Supremacy

"Law declared by Supreme Court shall be binding on all courts."

Result: CBDT circulars contrary to SC rulings are invalid. SC interpretation IS law.

Constitutional Remedies (Writs)

High Courts Article 226

For violation of law or rights (e.g., Lack of Jurisdiction, Natural Justice violation).

Supreme Court Article 32

Direct enforcement of Fundamental Rights.
