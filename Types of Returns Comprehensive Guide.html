Income Tax Returns (Filings)
Section 139 Series

Types of Income Tax Filings

A structured guide to Original, Belated, Revised, and Updated returns, including penalties and consequences.

Compliance • Correction • Enforcement
1

Original Return u/s 139(1)

Timely

The normal return filed within the prescribed due date. This is the ideal compliance scenario.

Due Dates

  • Individuals: 31 July
  • Audit Cases: 31 Oct
  • Transfer Pricing: 30 Nov

Benefits of Timely Filing

No Late Fee
Carry Forward Losses
Lower Interest
Full Deductions
2

Belated Return u/s 139(4)

Late

Filed after the due date but before the end of the Assessment Year (31st Dec). Better late than never, but it comes with a cost.

Mandatory Late Fee (234F)

Total Income Fee
Up to ₹5 Lakh ₹1,000
Above ₹5 Lakh ₹5,000

Major Consequences

  • Losses Denied: Business & Capital losses cannot be carried forward.
  • Interest: Section 234A interest applies for the delay.
3

Revised Return u/s 139(5)

Correction

Did you make a mistake? Omit income? Claim wrong TDS? This is your chance to fix it. Replaces the Original or Belated return.

  • Can revise Original (139(1)) AND Belated (139(4)).
  • Time Limit: 31st Dec of AY (same as belated).
  • No penalty if the error was bona fide.

Concept

"Replaces the previous return completely."

4

Updated Return u/s 139(8A)

Voluntary

A new concept allowing you to file even after the Belated/Revised deadline passed. Primarily to declare missed income and pay tax.

Conditions

  • Within 24 months from end of AY.
  • Only to declared additional income.
  • Cannot file to claim Refund.
  • Cannot file to increase Loss.

Additional Tax (Cost)

Within 12 months 25% extra
12 to 24 months 50% extra

*Calculated on tax + interest payable.

Defective Return 139(9)

Incomplete or inconsistent return (e.g., tax not paid, annexures missing).

Consequence: Must fix within 15 days. If not, treated as invalid (No return filed).

Return of Loss 139(3)

Filed to carry forward losses to future years.

Golden Rule: Must be filed by due date (31 July/31 Oct). Late filing = Loss disallowed (except House Property).

Master Concept

"Income-tax return filing is not a single event but a structured system allowing correction, compliance, enforcement, and voluntary disclosure—each with its own cost and consequence."
