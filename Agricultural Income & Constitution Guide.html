Can the Centre Tax Agriculture?

A common confusion: Why does the Income Tax Act talk about agriculture if the Centre cannot tax it?

ðŸ‘‰ The Short Answer:

The Centre CANNOT TAX agricultural income, but it CAN CONSIDER agricultural income for limited constitutional purposes.

1

Constitutional Division of Powers

Based on Article 246 & The Seventh Schedule of the Constitution.

State List (List II)

Entry 46

"Taxes on agricultural income."

  • Only States can levy this tax.
  • Parliament is barred from taxing it.

Union List (List I)

Entry 82

"Taxes on income other than agricultural income."

  • Central Income Tax = Non-Ag Only.

Why does the IT Act mention Agriculture?

Because "Mentioning" â‰  "Taxing".

Here are the 3 Constitutional Reasons:

1. To DEFINE Exclusion

Section 10(1): Exempts it.

To exempt something, the Act must first define it (Section 2(1A)) and set boundaries.

Without this, non-ag income would be misclassified to evade tax. Definition â‰  Taxation.

2. Partial Integration

Rate Purposes Only:

Ag income is used to determine the tax slab applicable to non-agricultural income.

This prevents rich agriculturists from paying lower tax rates on their other income. The Supreme Court upheld this.

3. Separate Mixed Income

Rule 7, 7A, 7B:

For incomes like Tea, Coffee, Rubber, the income is part agricultural, part business.

The Act splits this. The Ag part goes to the State; the Non-Ag part is taxed by the Centre.

What the Centre is ABSOLUTELY NOT allowed to do

Levy tax directly on Ag Income
Set tax rates on Ag Income
Collect Ag Income Tax
Enforce Ag Tax Compliance

Why is it in my ITR?

  • To identify exempt income (claim Section 10(1)).
  • To apply Partial Integration (if taxable income exists).
  • To prevent money laundering (hiding black money as Ag income).
  • To decide correct ITR form (e.g., ITR-1 limit is 5k).

Final Constitutional Position

Agricultural income is constitutionally outside the charging power of Parliament (Entry 46, List II). The Income-tax Act refers to it only for exclusion, classification, rate determination, and anti-evasion purposes.
