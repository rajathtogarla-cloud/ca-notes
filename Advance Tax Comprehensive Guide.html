What is Advance Tax?

Advance Tax is the payment of income tax in advance, during the financial year itself, rather than waiting until the year ends. It is based on your estimated income for the year.

Core Idea
Tax is paid as income accrues ("Pay-as-you-earn").
Purpose
Ensures continuous revenue for Govt & reduces year-end burden on you.

Liability Test

₹10k

Minimum Estimated Tax Liability

If tax payable (after TDS/TCS) is ₹10,000 or more, you MUST pay Advance Tax.

Who Pays?

Persons Covered

Advance tax applies to ALL assessees if the ₹10k limit is crossed.

Individuals Companies Firms/LLP HUF Trusts

Presumptive Taxation Exception (44AD / 44ADA)

Taxpayers opting for presumptive schemes don't need to pay quarterly. They can pay 100% of Advance Tax by 15th March.

Can I Revise?

Yes! Advance tax is based on estimation. If your income changes during the year:

  • You can increase subsequent instalments.
  • You can reduce subsequent instalments.

Interest on Default

Section 234B

Overall Shortfall

Applies if Advance Tax paid is less than 90% of Assessed Tax.

1% per month

From 1st April of AY till date of payment.

Section 234C

Deferment

Applies if instalments are not paid on time or are short paid.

1% per month

For 3 months (1 month for last instalment).
