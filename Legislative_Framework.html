"Federally Distributed, Constitutionally Bound"

Who makes tax law? It is not just Parliament. It is a federal distribution between the Union, the States, and the shared GST Council.

Federal Distribution of Tax Power

Article 246 divides Legislative Competence. It is not a monopoly.

Union Legislature

(Parliament)

  • Entry 82 Income Tax
  • Entry 83 Customs
  • Entry 84 Excise (Limited)
  • Art 248 Residuary
Exclusive power over Income Tax (except Agri).

State Legislature

(Vidhan Sabha)

  • Entry 46 Agri Income Tax
  • Entry 51 State Excise (Liquor)
  • Entry 49 Land & Building
  • Entry 52 Local Entry Tax
Why Agri income is exempt in IT Act? Because it's a State subject.

Shared Power

(GST Council)

  • Art 246A GST Power
  • Joint Parliament & States
  • Acts CGST, SGST, IGST
The only tax where legislative power is constitutionally concurrent.

Forms of Tax Legislation

The instruments used by both Parliament and State Assemblies.

A. Principal / Parent Statutes

Substantive

These create the charge, define the taxable event, and set the liability.

UNION: Income-tax Act, 1961 CGST Act
STATE: SGST Acts State Excise Acts

B. Annual Finance Acts

Annual Amendment

Passed every year. Parliament passes the Union Finance Act; States pass their own State Budgets/Acts.

  • Fixes Tax Rates & Slabs
  • Amends deductions/exemptions
  • Does NOT override Constitutional exclusions (e.g., Union Finance Act cannot tax Agriculture).

C. Money Bills

Democratic Control

Tax bills are Money Bills. Introduced only in the directly elected house.

Union Lok Sabha
State Vidhan Sabha

D. Treaty-based (DTAA)

International

Article 253. Only Parliament can enter international treaties. States cannot sign tax treaties with other nations.

Superpower: A DTAA can override Union domestic tax law.

Legislative Doctrines & Powers

Pith & Substance

True Nature Test

Used to resolve Union vs State conflicts. If the "substance" of a law falls under Entry 82 (Income), it is valid even if it incidentally touches State subjects.

Colourable Legislation

No Disguises Allowed

"What cannot be done directly, cannot be done indirectly." Parliament cannot tax agricultural income by disguising it as something else.

Retrospective Tax

The Power to Look Back

Parliament can enact tax laws retrospectively. However, it must be reasonable and not arbitrary (subject to Judicial Review).

Validation Acts

Curing Defects

If a court strikes down a tax, Parliament can pass a Validation Act to cure the specific defect (cause of invalidity) and make the tax valid again.

"Taxation in India is federally distributed, not centrally monopolized. Parliament and State Legislatures are co-sovereign within their constitutional fields."
