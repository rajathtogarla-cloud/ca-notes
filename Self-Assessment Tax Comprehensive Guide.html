What is Self-Assessment Tax?

Self-Assessment Tax (SAT) is the tax paid by the assessee after the end of the financial year but before filing the return. It bridges the gap between your final tax liability and what you've already paid via TDS/Advance Tax.

Why "Self"?
You assess your own income and voluntarily pay the shortfall.
Timing
Post-financial year, Pre-ITR filing.

When does it arise?

  • Advance Tax was insufficient
  • TDS deducted was low
  • New income discovered later
  • Interest or fees payable

Computation of SAT

The Formula

It's a simple subtraction game. Determine total liability, then subtract prepaid taxes.

Total Tax + Interest (A)
Prepaid Taxes (TDS/Adv) (B)
Self-Assessment Tax (A - B)

Step-by-Step Breakdown

  • 1. Compute Total Income & Tax
  • 2. Add Surcharge & Cess
  • 3. Add Interest (234A/B/C) & Fees (234F)
  • 4. Less: TDS, TCS, Advance Tax
  • 5. Pay Balance (SAT)

Default Consequences

Role of Form 26AS

Before computing SAT, you MUST verify Form 26AS/AIS. Only credits reflected there will be allowed. Mismatches lead to demand notices.

Always reconcile before paying!
