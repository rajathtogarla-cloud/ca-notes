Judicial Framework: Interpretation, Adjudication & Control

"Deciding what the law actually is"

Constitutional Position

Guardian of the Constitution

The Judiciary stands independent of the Legislature and Executive. It does not levy or administer tax, but it is the final arbiter of what tax statutes mean.

  • Independent Authority
  • Guardian of Rule of Law
  • Protector of Fundamental Rights

Key Articles

Art. 32 Writ Jurisdiction (Supreme Court)
Art. 226 Writ Jurisdiction (High Courts)
Art. 141 Law declared by SC is binding
Art. 136 Special Leave Petition (SLP)
"Judicial review is constitutionally indispensable to prevent the Executive from being a judge in its own cause."

1. Quasi-Judicial & 2. Tribunals

Direct Taxes (Income Tax)

Level 1: CIT (Appeals)

  • Departmental Authority
  • Can confirm, reduce, or annul assessment
  • Bound by CBDT circulars

Level 2: ITAT (Tribunal)

  • Independent Body (Ministry of Law)
  • Judicial & Accountant Members
  • Final authority on Facts
  • Not bound by CBDT circulars if against law
Indirect Taxes (GST/Customs)

Level 1: First Appellate Authority

  • Review adjudication orders
  • Decides on facts and law
  • Internal to the Department

Level 2: CESTAT / GSTAT

  • CESTAT: Customs, Excise, Service Tax
  • GSTAT: GST Appellate Tribunal
  • Independent of CBIC
  • Parallel to ITAT in function

3. Constitutional Courts

High Courts

Jurisdiction: Within the State

  • Article 226: Writ Jurisdiction against arbitrary actions.
  • Appeals: Admitted ONLY on a Substantial Question of Law.
  • No Fact Finding: Cannot re-appreciate evidence settled by Tribunal.
  • Binding Effect: Binding on all authorities within the state.

Supreme Court

Jurisdiction: Apex Court of India

  • Article 141: The law declared by SC is binding on all courts in India.
  • Article 136: Special Leave Petition (SLP) for exceptional cases.
  • Power: Can strike down tax laws, read down provisions, or validate retrospective amendments.
SC Judgment = The Law of the Land

Key Judicial Doctrines

Strict Interpretation

Tax must be clearly imposed by the letter of the law. If there is ambiguity, the benefit goes to the taxpayer.

Substance Over Form

Courts look at the real nature of the transaction, not just the label given to it by the parties.

Colourable Device

Tax planning is allowed, but artificial schemes designed solely to evade tax ("Colourable Devices") can be struck down.

Natural Justice

No tax demand without a fair hearing (Audi Alteram Partem) and a reasoned speaking order.
